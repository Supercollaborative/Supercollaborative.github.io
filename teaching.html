Right now, this is an outline of our plans. But the content and exercises will appear here soon too.

Format

The course is designed around the project, with two teaching streams to support it: planned content that we (collaboratively) create, and social content that is brought in during the course itself.

That said, we recognise that some students might just follow the teaching content, or the community, without taking part in a project. So we will ensure the course remains useful to these students too.

Content will be developed and posted here, in the open. This site is a GitHub repository allowing us to work with it using tickets, pull requests, etc.

The tentative plan is that when the course runs, this site transforms from the place where the course is built, to the place where it takes place. (By wiring the interaction we need into the site, in the manner of a static app)

First Three weeks

At the start of the course, we give the teams a small amount of code (setting up a project structure), and a mission. The students need to get and understand the code, and understand how to contribute changes to it. And that means there's a little bit of individual work before the project begins.

Critiques and demo

As the project progresses, there will be a number of studio style peer critiques. We find these are valuable both for the subject (receiving feedback) and the critic (learning from looking at others' work).

We'll also be maintaining a social stream where students and groups can post useful content into the course while it runs.
